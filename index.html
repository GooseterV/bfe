Glucose
Level 1
Glucose is a simple sugar consisting of 6 molecules of carbon, 12 molecules of hydrogen and 6 molecules of oxygen. It is an important source of energy for living organisms and is a building block for many carbohydrates.

Instructions: Drag each individual atom or molecule to it's respective spot on the larger molecule. Blue circles mean that there is multiple molecules/atoms that can be placed in the connected blue spaces. Click and drag to move the molecule. Right click to add another atom to the molecule. E.G: H1 -> H2. You can group atoms/smaller molecules together by doing the following: CTRL + LMB on the atom/molecule you want to group. Then, CTRL + LMB on the atom/molecule you want to group it with. You can click on more than 2 atoms and group them together. To finish grouping atoms, and create one bigger molecule, SHIFT + LMB on one of the grouped atoms. You will now be able to drag them all as one molecule. The lines represent single bonds and thicker lines represent double bonds.
